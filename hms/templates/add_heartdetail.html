Age

Description: Age of the patient in years.
Normal Range: N/A (used as-is).
Typical Values in Dataset: 29–77 years.

Sex

Description: Sex of the patient.
1 = Male
0 = Female
Normal Value: N/A (categorical).
Note: Used for risk stratification (men are typically at higher risk earlier in life).

Chest Pain Type (cp)

Description:
0 = Typical angina
1 = Atypical angina
2 = Non-anginal pain
3 = Asymptomatic
Normal (least concerning): 2 = Non-anginal pain.

Resting Blood Pressure (trestbps)

Description: Resting blood pressure (in mm Hg).
Normal Range: 90–120 mm Hg systolic.
Borderline: 120–129 (elevated), 130–139 (Stage 1 hypertension).

Serum Cholesterol (chol)

Description: Serum cholesterol in mg/dL.
Normal Range:
Desirable: less than 200 mg/dL
Borderline High: 200–239 mg/dL
High: ≥ 240 mg/dL

Fasting Blood Sugar (fbs)

Description: Fasting blood sugar > 120 mg/dL
1 = True
0 = False
Normal Value: 0 (i.e., lessthan 120 mg/dL)

Resting Electrocardiographic Results (restecg)

Values:
0 = Normal
1 = Having ST-T wave abnormality
2 = Left ventricular hypertrophy
Normal: 0

Maximum Heart Rate Achieved (thalach)

Description: Max heart rate achieved during exercise.
Typical Normal Range:
Varies with age: 220 – age (formula for maximum heart rate)
E.g., For 50 years old: max HR ~170 bpm
Higher values often indicate better heart fitness.

Exercise Induced Angina (exang)

Description:
1 = Yes
0 = No
Normal: 0 (no exercise-induced angina)

ST Depression Induced by Exercise (oldpeak)

Description: ST depression compared to rest.
Normal Range: 0.0–1.0
Values > 2.0 may indicate ischemia.

Slope of Peak Exercise ST Segment (slope)

Values:
0 = Upsloping (least risk)
1 = Flat
2 = Downsloping (highest risk)
Normal: 0

Number of Major Vessels Colored by Fluoroscopy (ca)

Range: 0–3 (or 4 in some datasets).
Normal: 0 (no major vessels with blockage)

Thalassemia (thal)

Values:
1 = Normal
2 = Fixed defect
3 = Reversible defect
Normal: 1

Target (Presence of Heart Disease)

Description: Diagnosis of heart disease (binary or multiclass in some versions).
0 = No disease
1 = Disease (in binary versions)
Normal: 0
